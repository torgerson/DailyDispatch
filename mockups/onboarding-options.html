Onboarding — Three Approaches

How do we teach new users the core loop (capture, task, tag, search) without making them read help pages?

Option A — Tooltip Walkthrough (5 Steps)
Focused tooltips appear one at a time, pointing at the specific UI element they're teaching. A pulsing outline highlights the target. Each step has a clear action: "Try it" or "Next." Progress dots show where you are. Can be dismissed at any point with "Skip tour." Feels like Notion's or Linear's first-run experience.

Option B — Getting Started Checklist
A floating card in the top-right corner with 5 tasks to complete. Each item has a short description. Items check off as you do them (auto-detected). Progress bar fills up. The card can be minimized or dismissed permanently. Feels like Stripe's or Vercel's dashboard onboarding. Non-blocking — you can use the app freely while the checklist sits there.

Option C — Inline Coach Cards in the Feed
Teaching cards appear directly in the feed, mixed with your content. Each card teaches one concept with a clear call-to-action. Cards dismiss individually (x button) or all at once. They feel like part of the content, not a modal overlay. As you complete each action, the next card appears. Feels like Slack's or GitHub's contextual tips.
